The Visual Drift

I've always held onto images that inspire me—a habit that began in the golden era of Tumblr, before Instagram was even an idea.

During the endless 2020 COVID lockdowns, I decided to print those images and assemble them into a giant mood board. It was something to do during those long stay-at-home stretches, and something interesting to look at while stuck in a makeshift home office for hours each day.

Years later it's still there. The images are now faded and falling down, but it's a visual reminder of that strange time that I have struggled to part with. So I set out to preserve what I built in a format that will never fade: a digital version that can continue to grow and be accessed by anyone. The Visual Drift was born.

The Visual Drift is an interactive visual record of interest.
A case study of visual culture through my lens.
An always-evolving expression of my personal taste in fine art, design, typography, and content.

A mood board is not a new or unique idea. But after decades of collecting thousands of images—most of which are buried away on random hard drives—I've finally found a method to compile, catalogue, and continue a project that was created to help me find inspiration. In doing so, I've found that it just might inspire you too.
